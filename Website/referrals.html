Referrals

Making a referral is simple and straightforward

We welcome referrals from individuals, families, healthcare providers, social workers, case managers, and other community partners. Our goal is to provide seamless support from the moment a referral is made.

How to Make a Referral

Making a referral to Anchor Point Supports is simple and straightforward. We accept referrals through multiple channels to ensure accessibility for everyone.

Online Referral

Complete our secure online referral form available on our contact page. This is the fastest way to submit a referral and receive a response.

Phone Referral

Call us at (555) 123-4567 to speak with one of our intake coordinators who will guide you through the referral process.

Email Referral

Send referral information to referrals@anchorpointsupports.com. Include basic contact information and service needs.

Professional Referral

Healthcare providers and case managers can use our professional referral portal for streamlined communication and coordination.

What Happens After a Referral?

  • Initial Contact: We will reach out within 24-48 hours to acknowledge receipt of the referral
  • Assessment: Schedule a comprehensive needs assessment to understand individual requirements
  • Service Planning: Develop a personalized support plan in collaboration with the individual and family
  • Implementation: Begin services with ongoing monitoring and adjustment as needed
  • Follow-up: Regular communication to ensure satisfaction and address any concerns

Who Can Benefit from Our Services?

We support individuals of all ages with intellectual and developmental disabilities, including:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Down Syndrome
  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Other developmental disabilities

Our Mission

My mission is simple: to be a reliable, compassionate support for individuals and families who need help navigating everyday life and important transitions.

About

Anchor Point Supports, LLC provides compassionate care and support for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities.
